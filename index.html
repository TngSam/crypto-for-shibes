Essentials

I think we'll start from the essentials. Like the price movement and why exactly price is moving. Traders buy and sell an asset every second. For example, you need to buy DOGE. First of all, you need to place an order to buy. You can choose at what price you want to buy it (limit order) and also you can buy it immediately at market price (market order). Let's specify the price at which you want to buy DOGE, i.e. $0,35. You press the 'Buy' button and your order will be placed in the orderbook and it will be executed when the price will hit $0,35.
What's an orderbook? â€” To understand people's intentions there exist such a cool tool as an orderbook.

Let's break down what the orderbook consists of.
In the first column you can see the price at which orders are placed. Second column tells us how much DOGE people want to buy or sell at this price. And the third column shows the cumulative amount of orders placed. Cumulative means that orders at previous levels are summed up at each level. When the amount of DOGE at the level closest to the price decreases, it means that people are whether buying or canceling orders, and vice versa with selling. Simply put, it's called absorbing.

Okay, we finished with the orderbook. Now let's take a look at OHLC chart.

What's the OHLC?

OHLC is standing for Open, High, Low and Close.

OHLC chart consists of these candles. Each candle have an abstract timeframe that you chose. For example, in this screenshot I used 4h timeframe so every candle closes every 4 hours. Each candle has: open price â€” the price at which candle was opened; high price shows the highest price in the particular timeframe; low price wick shows the lowest price; close price shows at which price candle was closed. Usually, if the candle body is green then close price is higher than open price, and if the candle body is red â€” vice versa.
New candle's open price is equal to previous candle close price.

Volume

An important part in price analysis is volume. The volume shows the total amount of shares traded in particular timeframe. In most cases you can observe volume that was traded in each candle (Open-High-Low-Close-Volume chart. OHLCV).

Why it's important? Well, in a nutshell, if any every big move whether it's a bull run or correction is not backed up by increasing volume then there is a high probability that the market will go in reverse direction soon. A huge amount of the volume at the particular candle usually means that we're breaking some important price level so people are hyperactive.

Price action

Every asset has such a price action as growth and only up in big steps and suddenly price is starting to fall in bloody hell. These moves are called "bull run" and "bear market" accordingly. They have different names as well, but for the sake of simplicity, we'll call them like I said. Let's take a look at the recent DOGE price action.

Familiar structure, isn't it? DOGE performed +60% and then dropped -20%. Yes, that's exactly what I was talking about. We see a little bull run and a bit of correction. Why did I said little? Well, usually people say 'bull run cycle has begun' and they mean global and larger timeframes (like months). Because the true bull run usually lasts months or sometimes even years, bear market too.
